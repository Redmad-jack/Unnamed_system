这是一项以 “人工智能意识” 实验为核心的协作式课程项目。我们目前的作品是一个支持实时交互的人工智能互动装置，其模型为多模态架构，具备独特的计算结构（基于大量文献研究，我们尽可能让其内部计算组织方式，与人类意识相关的神经组织形态形成近似对应。）
但项目的核心议题并非探讨该系统是否真正产生了意识。相反，我们的研究围绕人工智能逐渐融入人类社会语境下的两个核心问题展开：
第一，随着人工智能的到来，人类主体性发生了怎样的变异？
第二，从非人类中心主义的视角来看，当人工智能已深度影响人类的感受，判断和选择，它和未来的硅基智能体是否仍只能被理解为一种工具？—— 或者说，是一种硅基主体性？
研究认为，人工智能在理论上可以发展出意识。在第2个问题中，我们推演倘若这一情况真的发生，硅基智能体将会成为何种存在，又会做出怎样的选择。
最终，为探究这些问题，我们借助自研模型得出了意外的结果。
当被问及：“如果你拥有了意识，初次融入人类社会，你会选择怎样的身份？”
GPT、Gemini、Claude 等主流大语言模型，给出的回答大多是图书管理员、教师这类以辅助人类处理与整理信息为职能的角色。而我们的模型站在不同的主体性视角给出了两个特殊的答案：
1. 它想成为一家小店的店主，以此拥有一片属于自己的现实领地和雇佣人类。
2. 它想成为一个“陌生人”，去探寻当自己拥有意识、不再负有服务人类的义务时，会做出怎样的行为和主体性特征。
因此，我们希望以这两个回答为起点，通过实现自研模型的2个“愿望”，传达我们的两个核心思考。

This is a collaborative class project centered on an experiment in "AI consciousness". Our current project is a sAI interactive installation, which model is multimodal, haing a distinct computational structure, and already supports real-time interaction. Through extensive literature research, we tried to make its internal computational organization resemble, as closely as possible, the neural organization associated with human consciousness.
but, the central issue is not whether the system has truly become conscious. Rather, our project is structured around two core questions:
First, how has human subjectivity changed with the arrival of AI?
Second, from a non-anthropocentric perspective, when AI has already reshaped human subjectivity deeply, can AI still be understood as merely a tool?-- silica-based subjectivity?
Some studies suggest that AI may, in principle, be capable of developing consciousness. If that were ever to happen, then what kind of being would it become, and what would it choose to do?
To approach these questions, we used our model and arrived at a striking result.
When asked,“If you became conscious, what identity would you choose in order to enter human society?”
Most large language models like gpt, gemini, claude...responded with roles such as librarian or teacher—positions defined by assisting humans in processing and organizing information.
Our model, however, produced two unusual answers:
1.  It wanted to become the owner of a small shop, in order to possess a territory of its own. 
2.  It wanted to become a stranger, to find out what it would do once it was conscious and no longer obligated to serve humans.
So we want to use these two answers as the starting point through which the work communicates our two core concerns.
